Learning plan

My learning plan is aimed at improving my technical skills, productivity, and overall well-being. I will be working with a team of experts and advisors to help me learn and grow. Bellow, you could find my objectives and limitations. Further to that I have created A step by step guide to this Learning Plane.

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I aim to establish myself as an independent IT expert, becoming a full-stack developer, software engineer, and DevOps specialist. This would enable me to work flexibly from any location and across different time zones.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

My strengths include strong problem-solving skills, adaptability, and resilience. I'm always willing to learn new skills and have excellent communication and teamwork abilities. I also possess good analytical skills and experience in Shell scripting, RDBMS, Java, and the Spring framework. Additionally, I have a strong work ethic and a commitment to continuous learning.

However, my limitations include poor time management and organizational skills, which I am actively working on improving.

What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would definitely like to improve my focus, time management, and ability to avoid distractions. Additionally, I believe I need to work on presenting ideas and information more confidently.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:

  • An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives
  • What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

I've committed to this program because I want to turn the challenging job market situation (struggling to find a job) into an opportunity to upskill and learn new things. I've also set some personal goals for myself, like learning front-end web development and improving my communication skills.

Even though I should dedicate at least 8 hours a day to study, I have significant responsibilities to my family and need to deal with our financial situation on daily basis, which takes away from my study time. On top of that, I need to drop my kids off at school every morning, so I'm available from 9:30 a.m. at the earliest. Sometimes, I also have to pick them up, meaning I'm unavailable between 2:45 and 3:30 p.m.

The key for me is to plan ahead based on my goals—daily, weekly, and long-term. I try to reflect on my progress and adjust my goals as needed, communicating this with my team and facilitator. Time management is crucial, as I often lose track of time or forget to take breaks. I try to stick to the timeboxing strategy and set alarms (using the Pomodoro technique).

If things start to get overwhelming, I'd need to pause for a while, have thing about what's going on and what next steps should I take. Ultimately, I'll reach out to my facilitator and use the other available resources for help.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

As mentioned earlier, if I find that I need help (that is a hardest bit, to admit that.... similar to the situation of boiling frog), I will reach out to my cohort facilitator or any other available facilitator. I will also seek assistance from my cohort peers, either in person or through the available electronic communication channels.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I hope the Facilitation team will support me through my challenging personal situation and help guide me away from negative thoughts that could hinder my learning, progress, and ability to meet deadlines. I expect them to steer me in the right direction on my learning journey and provide honest, constructive feedback.

In addition, I expect them to maintain confidentiality and be committed to helping me develop my skills. They should foster a positive and supportive learning environment while offering guidance and support throughout the process. They will be there to listen, answer questions, and provide advice based on my strengths, weaknesses, and goals. I also expect them to help me identify potential roadblocks and offer resources to overcome them.
